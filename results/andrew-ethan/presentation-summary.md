# Presentation Notes

## Core story

- Relative to `market`, the practice-based policy improves final average environmental quality from 0.029 to 0.124.
- Relative to `productivist`, the same policy avoids the environmental collapse visible in the baseline (-1.000 versus 0.124).
- The practice-based policy shifts the final land-use mix to I=54.90%, O=9.50%, S=35.60%.

## Economic interpretation

- Mean farmer profit before subsidy moves from 1.179 under `market` to 1.415 under the practice policy.
- Mean subsidy paid per farmer per period is 0.385, so the after-subsidy profit rises to 1.801.
- Only 78.67% of farmers are weakly above their market baseline before subsidy transfers, compared with 100.00% after transfers.

## Scope choice

- The main presentation should focus on `productivist`, `market`, and `policy_practice`.
- `policy_conversion` and `policy_results` are retained as robustness checks, not headline evidence.
